Which gives more work per dollar?

Unanswerable in general — and anyone giving you a flat answer is guessing. Both vendors moved limits three-plus times in 2026 (Anthropic doubled 5-hour windows in May and is running a weekly boost right now; OpenAI lifted the 5-hour limit entirely after the GPT-5.6 Sol launch). The honest heuristics:

  • On the OpenAI side, model choice is the big lever — the credit spread between models is ~5×, so dropping to a mid-tier model for routine work stretches the week dramatically.
  • On the Anthropic side, the Opus budget is the scarce resource — Sonnet headroom is much deeper, so reserving Opus for hard problems is the equivalent lever.
  • Promotions are real but dated — never plan capacity around a boost that expires next week.

The two-provider workflow

A growing pattern (ask any "what do I do now that I'm rate-limited" thread): run both subscriptions, treat them as one pool of agent-hours. One hits a wall mid-task — you hand the branch to the other. $40/month for Claude Pro + ChatGPT Plus buys more effective throughput than $100 on either single plan, at the cost of one annoyance: you now have four quotas to track (two windows × two providers), in two different UIs, with two different reset schedules.

That bookkeeping is the entire reason AI Status Bar exists: every account of both providers in one menu, each with its 5-hour and weekly percentage and reset clock, with alerts before either wall. The switch decision becomes a glance.

FAQ

Can I use one subscription's quota from the other tool?

No — quotas are per-vendor and per-account. The two-provider workflow means two subscriptions; what you pool is your own workflow, not the billing.

Which one should I subscribe to first?

Whichever agent you prefer working with — limits shouldn't drive that choice at $20. The two-provider setup is for when you're already hitting one vendor's weekly cap regularly.

Do both really use a 5-hour window?

Yes, it's convergent design — though as of July 2026 OpenAI has temporarily suspended theirs, leaving only the weekly cap. Expect it back.
